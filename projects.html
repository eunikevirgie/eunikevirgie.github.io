“New Normal”: Singapore’s Tourism Recovery After COVID-19

by Virgie Yuliana

HOME | LinkedIn | GitHub


Aims

This project examines how Singapore’s tourism sector recovered from the COVID-19 shock between 2019 and 2025, and whether the structure of visitor markets has returned to its pre-pandemic pattern or shifted to a new normal. Using seven interactive visualisations, the analysis tracks recovery dynamics over time, compares performance across source markets, and explores structural changes in Singapore’s tourism economy.

The Overall Recovery Path

International visitor arrivals collapsed sharply in early 2020 and remained near zero through much of 2021. While arrivals rebounded after borders reopened in 2022, indexed comparisons show that total arrivals have not consistently returned to pre-pandemic levels, indicating an incomplete and volatile recovery.

Data: Monthly international visitor arrivals by place of residence are retrieved from Singapore Department of Statistics (SingStat). I clean and reshape the data in Python (Google Colab), group markets into regions, and construct a 2019=100 index before exporting a tidy CSV for Vega-Lite.
